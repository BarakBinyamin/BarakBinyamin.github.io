Exploration into the Tile

A path to accesing a Tiles features

Getting Started

The Tile is a device that helps a person locate their valuebles. It works by playing a tune to help the consumer find their lost item. Tile (the company) maintains that only the Tile owner can access it's features, nonetheless it's still good fun to try.

Image from https://www.thetileapp.com/en-us

Gathering Info

One good way to learn about a device is by testing its inputs and outputs. Since we know that the tile is a bluetooth-low-energy device, we can use bluetoothctl to do just that.

If you are unfamiliar with blutoothctl, here's some reading that may help

The Data

After staring a scan (scan on), five Tiles were instantly recognizable

Notice the alias, "Tile", next to five of the MAC- adresses

RSSI is a measurement of signal strength, this can be used to approximate distance. Notice the ServiceData value

After taking a look into the lines with [CHG], it was found that "ManufacturerData Key: 0x004c" indicates that a device is from apple. What always followed a "ManufacturerData Key: 0x004c" was a "ManufacturerData value:" and some set of characters. This is possibly communication between the Tile and a nearby apple device...
